About

Researcher at the interface of disciplines.

I am a researcher with a background in life sciences, working at the intersection of biology, engineering, and software. My work has included microbiology, molecular biology, plant physiology, population evolution, analytical chemistry, and feedback-control systems.

I focus on understanding complex experimental systems, improving their reliability, and building practical tools that make experiments reproducible and easier to execute.

M.Sc. Thesis - Weizmann Institute of Science

Stabilizing Stochastic Phenotypes Using Real-Time Feedback Control

Investigated whether an external real-time feedback system could influence phenotypic distributions within a yeast population. The platform combined automated fluorescence microscopy, microfluidics, temperature control, live-cell imaging, and real-time image analysis.

  • Integrated software components into a single operational workflow.
  • Built automation for image analysis and experiment management.
  • Processed approximately 20,000 images per week-long experiment.
  • Conducted approximately 20 complete experiments; 9 yielded full datasets.

First Author Publication

Simulating Martian Atmospheric Conditions in Plants

Assembled and calibrated an Arduino-based environmental control system integrating temperature, humidity, CO2, nitrogen sensing, and gas-valve regulation. The optimized platform operated continuously for approximately three months.

Microbial Ecology

Natural Wine Fermentation

Planned sampling from leaves, fruit, roots, soil, fermenting wine, and aged wine. Performed DNA extractions and prepared samples for 16S rRNA Illumina sequencing.

Industrial Pilot Project

Chemical Identification of Metals in Meteorites

Evaluated the scientific feasibility and reproducibility of a trace-metal recovery workflow using controlled digestion, salt conversion, product recovery, and comparison to external lab analysis.
